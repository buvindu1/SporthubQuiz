Australia national cricket team

As of 2021, Australia is the only team to win five Cricket World Cups; no other team has won more than two. The national team has played 853 Test matches, winning 405, losing 229, drawing 217 and tying 2. As of May 2022, Australia is ranked first in the ICC Test Championship on 128 rating points. The national team has played 174 Twenty20 International matches, winning 91, losing 76, tying 3 and with 4 ending in a no-result. As of May 2022, Australia is ranked fifth in the ICC T20I Championship on 251 rating points.

India national cricket team

The Indian cricket team has won several accolades over the years. Some of their notable achievements include winning the ICC Cricket World Cup in 1983 and 2011, winning the ICC World T20 in 2007, being Number-One in ICC Test ranking in 2009, and winning the Asia Cup five times.

New Zealand national cricket team

The New Zealand national cricket team represents New Zealand in men’s international cricket. They played their first Test in 1930 against England in Christchurch, becoming the fifth country to play Test cricket. From 1930 New Zealand had to wait until 1956, more than 26 years, for its first Test victory, against the West Indies at Eden Park in Auckland. The team is also known as the Black Caps. New Zealand has won several accolades over the years. Some of their notable achievements include winning the ICC World Test Championship in 2021, reaching the final of the ICC Cricket World Cup in 2015 and 2019, and winning the ICC Champions Trophy in 2000.

England cricket team

The England cricket team represents England and Wales in international cricket. The team is governed by the England and Wales Cricket Board (ECB). The team played its first Test match in 1877 against Australia. England has won four ICC Cricket World Cup titles (1975, 1979, 1987, and 2019) and one ICC World Twenty20 (2010). They have also been runners-up in two ICC Cricket World Cups (1979 and 1992) and two ICC World Twenty20s (2016 and 2021).

Carlos Alcaraz

Carlos Alcaraz Garfia is a Spanish professional tennis player who is currently ranked as the world No. 1 player in men’s singles by the Association of Tennis Professionals (ATP). He has won eight ATP Tour-level singles titles, including the 2022 US Open and three Masters 1000 titles. With his US Open win, Alcaraz became the youngest man to top the singles rankings at 19 years old.

Novak Djokovic

Novak Djokovic is a Serbian professional tennis player who has won an all-time record 22 Grand Slam men’s singles titles, jointly-held with Rafael Nadal. He has been the world No. 1 for a record 380 weeks, and the year-end world No. 1 a record seven times. Djokovic is one of eight male players to achieve the Career Grand Slam and one of two players to achieve it on three different surfaces. He has also won more than 6 ATP Masters 1000 titles in a single year.

Stefanos Tsitsipas

Stefanos Tsitsipas is a Greek professional tennis player who has been ranked as high as world No. 3 by the Association of Tennis Professionals (ATP), which he first achieved on 9 August 2021. He has won 6 ATP singles titles and reached his first Grand Slam final at the 2021 French Open. He also won the 2021 ATP Finals.

Casper Ruud

Casper Ruud is a Norwegian professional tennis player who has been ranked as high as world No. 4 by the Association of Tennis Professionals (ATP), which he first achieved on 12 September 2022. He has won 9 ATP Tour singles titles, eight of which were on clay courts.

Evgeny Rylov

Evgeny Rylov is a Russian competitive swimmer and Olympic champion specializing in backstroke events. He won three gold medals at the 2014 Summer Youth Olympics in Nanjing, and a bronze medal at his senior international debut at the 2015 World Championships. He won two gold medals and one silver medal at the 2020 Tokyo Olympics

Kliment Kolesnikov

Kliment Kolesnikov is a Russian swimmer who holds world junior records in eight events: 50/100/200 back in long course, as well as 200 free, 50/100/200 back and 100 IM in short course. He won seven medals, including six gold, at the Youth Olympic Games in 2018. He won a silver medal and a bronze medal at the 2020 Tokyo Olympics

Ryan Murphy

Ryan Murphy is an American competitive swimmer specializing in backstroke. He is a four-time Olympic gold medalist and the former world-record holder in the men’s 100-meter backstroke. Murphy’s international breakthrough came in 2011 when he won the Bronze medal in 200m Backstroke at the World Junior Championships. The following year, he won the 4x100m Medley title and a Bronze medal in 200m Backstroke at the World Swimming Championships. He has an impressive collection of 25 international medals in swimming, of which 13 are gold. Murphy has also won several awards and honors including Male Athlete of the Year at Golden Goggle Awards in 2018.

Thomas Ceccon

Thomas Ceccon is an Italian swimmer who is a world record holder in the long course 100 metre backstroke and the short course 4×100 metre freestyle relay. He is also the Italian record holder in the long course 50 metre backstroke and 50 metre butterfly. He is the 2022 World champion in the 100 metre backstroke and 2022 European champion in the 100 metre backstroke.

Viktor Axelsen

Viktor Axelsen is a Danish badminton player who is the reigning World and Olympic Champion in Men’s singles, having won the 2022 World Championships and 2020 Olympics. He is a three-time European champion. He has won two BWF World Championships Gold Medals (2017, 2022), two YONEX-SUNRISE India Open Champion (2017, 2019), two Dubai BWF World Super Series Finals Champion (2016, 2017), 2022 French Open Champion, 2022 Indonesia Masters Champion, and 2022 Indonesia Open Champion

Jonatan Christie

Jonatan Christie is an Indonesian badminton player who won his first senior international title at the age of 15 by defeating his compatriot Alamsyah Yunus at the Indonesia International. He won the men’s singles title at the 2017 Southeast Asian Games and at the 2018 Asian Games. He was part of Indonesia’s winning team at the 2020 Thomas Cup. His first major international achievement came in 2013 when he won the Silver medal in the Mixed Team event at the World Junior Championships

Anthony Sinisuka Ginting

Anthony Sinisuka Ginting is an Indonesian badminton player who won bronze medal at the 2018 Asian Games and at the 2020 Olympics in the men’s singles event. He became the first Youth Olympic badminton medalist to win a medal at the Olympics, having also won bronze in 2014.

Lee Zii Jia

Lee Zii Jia is a Malaysian badminton player who is currently ranked 4 in the world. He was the men’s singles gold medalist at the 2019 Southeast Asian Games and won his first BWF Super 1000 title at the 2021 All England Open. Lee is the reigning men’s singles Asian champion, winning the title at the 2022 Badminton Asia Championships.
